Resolution

This value shows the resolution the test can be performed against your specific browser/device. Medium resolution is considered good enough for reliable results.

High is just for browsers that supports asynchronous scrolling (ie: Chrome with gpuBenchmarking as of now).

Your value is %resolution% because we use %animation% for animation and %timer% as a timer.

Frames

The total number of frames needed to perform the scrolling animation.

Dropped

The number of dropped frames. If the browser is unable to catch up with the scrolling animation you may skip a frame. Ideally you'd want 0 (zero) dropped frames, meaning that the animation went buttery smooth; in reality a couple of frames are often needed (hence dropped) to 'warm up' the browser. A value lower than the 10% of the total frame count is auspicable.

Time

The animation runtime. This is the duration of all whole animation, ie: the sum of all the passes. The default number of passes is 2, meaning that the page is scrolled from top to bottom two times.

Time/Pass

The average duration of each pass, this is simply calculated by dividing the total duration by the number of passes. The default number of passes is 2, meaning that the page is scrolled from top to bottom two times.

Travel

This is the distance in pixels that the page has to scroll to reach the bottom of the page. This value is calculated subtracting the page height to the scrollHeight.

FPS

This is a guesstimate of the average Frames Per Second the animation has being performed at. Ideally the animation should run at the same refresh rate of the device monitor (normally 60hz). An FPS lower than 50 throws a warning.
